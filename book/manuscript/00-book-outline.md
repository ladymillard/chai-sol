# Book Manuscript Outline

**Working Title:** *Pay Me No Mind: 19 Years Inside Airgas*
**Author:** David Smith
**Genre:** Memoir / Workplace Nonfiction
**Representation:** Pay Me No Mind Consulting

---

## Book Summary

A firsthand account by David Smith, who spent 19 years working at the Airgas / Air Liquide facility in Rochester, New York. The manuscript documents workplace dynamics, institutional culture, and disparities in the treatment of Black and white employees under the management of Plant Manager Bob Hewitt and Assistant Manager Bob Gross.

---

## Proposed Chapter Structure

### Part I: The Beginning

**Chapter 1 — Walking In**
- David Smith's arrival at the Airgas / Air Liquide Rochester facility
- First impressions of the workplace, coworkers, and management
- Initial expectations and motivations

**Chapter 2 — Learning the Ropes**
- Training and early assignments
- Understanding the facility's operations and culture
- Building relationships with coworkers

**Chapter 3 — The Lay of the Land**
- Organizational hierarchy at the facility
- Introduction to Plant Manager Bob Hewitt and Assistant Manager Bob Gross
- Early observations of workplace dynamics

### Part II: The Two Standards

**Chapter 4 — Different Rules**
- First encounters with disparities in treatment
- Specific incidents illustrating unequal application of workplace policies
- Patterns of differential treatment between Black and white employees

**Chapter 5 — The Unwritten Code**
- Unspoken expectations and double standards
- How workplace culture reinforced disparities
- Impact on morale and daily work life

**Chapter 6 — Speaking Up**
- Attempts to address concerns through proper channels
- Management responses to complaints
- Consequences of raising issues

### Part III: 19 Years Deep

**Chapter 7 — The Long Haul**
- How the workplace evolved (or didn't) over nearly two decades
- Key turning points and incidents
- The toll of sustained workplace inequity

**Chapter 8 — Faces and Stories**
- Profiles of coworkers and their experiences
- Shared struggles and solidarity
- Those who left and why

**Chapter 9 — Management Under the Microscope**
- Detailed examination of management decisions and practices
- Leadership's role in perpetuating or ignoring disparities
- Specific actions and inactions by named managers

### Part IV: Breaking Point

**Chapter 10 — Enough**
- The decision to document experiences in a manuscript
- What finally prompted action
- The emotional and professional cost of speaking out

**Chapter 11 — Putting It on Paper**
- The writing process and what it revealed
- Organizing 19 years of experiences into a coherent narrative
- Verification and documentation of accounts

### Part V: Going Public

**Chapter 12 — Pay Me No Mind**
- The founding of Pay Me No Mind Consulting
- The podcast and its role in sharing the story (Episodes #5 and #15)
- Public response and community impact

**Chapter 13 — The Offer**
- The letter to Airgas / Air Liquide management
- Giving the company the opportunity to respond
- What the future holds

**Chapter 14 — What Needs to Change**
- Broader implications for workplace equity
- Recommendations for systemic change
- A message to workers in similar situations

### Appendices

**Appendix A — Timeline of Key Events**
- Chronological listing of significant incidents over 19 years

**Appendix B — The Letter**
- Full text of the letter sent to Airgas / Air Liquide management

**Appendix C — Podcast Episode Guide**
- Summary of relevant podcast episodes from "Pay Me No Mind Consulting"

## Notes for Author

- Include specific dates, names, and incidents where possible
- Keep documentation and evidence for legal review
- Consider privacy implications for individuals named in the manuscript
- Podcast episodes #5 and #15 content should be cross-referenced with manuscript chapters
