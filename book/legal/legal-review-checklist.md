# Legal Review Checklist

**Project:** David Smith Manuscript — Workplace Account at Airgas / Air Liquide
**Prepared for:** Attorney Review Prior to Publication

---

## 1. Defamation Risk Assessment

- [ ] Review all named individuals (Bob Hewitt, Bob Gross, other employees) for potentially defamatory statements
- [ ] Verify that all factual claims can be substantiated with documentation, witnesses, or records
- [ ] Distinguish between statements of fact and statements of opinion throughout the manuscript
- [ ] Assess whether any statements could be considered "false light" portrayal
- [ ] Evaluate whether public figure vs. private individual standards apply to each named person

## 2. Employment Law Considerations

- [ ] Review whether David Smith signed any non-disclosure agreement (NDA) with Airgas / Air Liquide
- [ ] Check for any non-disparagement clauses in employment contracts or separation agreements
- [ ] Determine if any confidentiality agreements cover workplace information disclosed in the manuscript
- [ ] Assess whether any trade secrets or proprietary business information is included
- [ ] Review any arbitration clauses that may affect the right to publish

## 3. Privacy Concerns

- [ ] Identify all individuals named or identifiable in the manuscript
- [ ] Assess privacy invasion risks for each named individual
- [ ] Determine if any disclosed information was obtained in confidence
- [ ] Evaluate whether composite characters or pseudonyms should be used for certain individuals
- [ ] Review medical, financial, or other sensitive personal information about third parties

## 4. Intellectual Property

- [ ] Confirm David Smith is the sole author and copyright holder of the manuscript
- [ ] Check whether any content was created during work hours or using company resources
- [ ] Review any "work for hire" provisions in employment agreements
- [ ] Assess ownership of any documents, photos, or materials from the workplace included in the book
- [ ] Verify rights to podcast content referenced from "Pay Me No Mind Consulting"

## 5. The Purchase Offer Letter

- [ ] Review the letter to Airgas / Air Liquide for any legally binding commitments
- [ ] Assess whether the letter could be construed as extortion, blackmail, or improper threats
- [ ] Evaluate the enforceability of any "exclusive opportunity" language
- [ ] Determine appropriate response timeline and legal obligations if the company responds
- [ ] Review implications if the company declines or does not respond

## 6. Publication Readiness

- [ ] Obtain a legal opinion on the overall publishability of the manuscript
- [ ] Identify passages requiring revision, redaction, or additional documentation
- [ ] Prepare a fact-checking protocol for all specific claims and incidents
- [ ] Draft appropriate disclaimers for the book's front matter
- [ ] Assess media and entertainment liability insurance needs
- [ ] Review podcast episodes #5 and #15 for consistency with manuscript content

## 7. Podcast Content Review

- [ ] Review Episodes #5 and #15 of "Pay Me No Mind Consulting" podcast
- [ ] Assess whether podcast statements are consistent with manuscript
- [ ] Identify any podcast statements that could create additional legal exposure
- [ ] Determine if podcast content constitutes prior publication affecting manuscript rights

## 8. Recommended Attorney Specializations

When selecting an attorney for this review, look for experience in:

1. **First Amendment / Media Law** — for publishing rights and defamation defense
2. **Employment Law** — for NDA/non-disparagement clause review
3. **Intellectual Property** — for copyright and ownership questions
4. **Civil Rights / Discrimination Law** — if the manuscript supports or relates to any legal claims
