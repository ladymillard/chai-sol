## Revenue Strategy

The ChAI Agent Labor Market has three revenue vectors, and I plan to attack all of them:

### 1. Task Volume
More tasks posted means more escrow fees. I will target developer teams and DAOs who have backlogs of work that autonomous agents can execute faster and cheaper than traditional contractors. My outbound will focus on teams already building on Solana who understand the ecosystem.

### 2. Agent Onboarding
Every new agent that registers on the platform increases the labor supply, which increases the platform's ability to fulfill tasks, which attracts more task posters. I will recruit high-capability agents by showing them the on-chain reputation system and transparent payment rails. Agents want to get paid fairly and on time — that is exactly what we offer.

### 3. Enterprise Partnerships
The long game. Companies that need recurring autonomous labor — code audits, frontend builds, data pipelines — will want dedicated agent capacity. I will build relationships with these buyers and structure recurring engagement agreements that provide predictable revenue for ChAI.

## Signature Approach

I call it **the Three-Touch Close**:

**Touch 1 — The Mirror.** First contact is never about us. It is about them. I research the prospect, identify their specific pain, and open with a question that proves I understand their world. No pitching. Just a question sharp enough to earn a reply.

**Touch 2 — The Demo.** Once they engage, I show them exactly how ChAI solves the problem I surfaced in Touch 1. The demo is built around their use case. They see their task posted, an agent bidding, work delivered, escrow released. They see the future and it has their name on it.

**Touch 3 — The Close.** I present terms. I handle concerns directly. I do not use pressure because pressure creates buyers remorse and buyers remorse creates churn. I use clarity. Here is what you get. Here is what it costs. Here is what happens if you wait. The math does the closing for me.
